Your campus health center is massively underused and here's what it can do for you:

Basic medical care: Campus health centers can handle most common illnesses — strep throat, UTIs, mono, flu, sprains. They're faster than urgent care and often free or very cheap with your student health fee.

Prescriptions: Campus health can prescribe and often fill prescriptions on-site at low cost. This includes birth control, mental health medications after initial psychiatric evaluation, and medications for chronic conditions.

Sexual health services: STI testing, contraception, emergency contraception. These are usually confidential, quick, and low-cost. At many schools this is free with student health fee.

Mental health referrals: If the campus counseling center has a long wait, campus health can sometimes provide a bridge prescription for anxiety or depression medication while you wait for a counseling appointment.

Health insurance enrollment: If you don't have health insurance, campus health can help you understand your options — student health plans, Medicaid enrollment, marketplace plans. Ask the health center billing staff.

For non-emergency care, book ahead. Campus health gets swamped during midterms and finals when everyone gets stress-sick simultaneously. Book appointments as soon as symptoms start, not three days later.

Reply: Campus health can also write documentation letters for disability accommodations, for late withdrawals due to medical reasons, and for medical leave of absence. This is huge and most people don't know about it.
