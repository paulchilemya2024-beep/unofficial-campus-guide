So my financial aid was cut by $4,000 this year and I was completely blindsided. Here's what worked for me when I appealed:

First, write a formal appeal letter. Be specific — don't just say "I need more money." Explain exactly what changed in your financial situation. I listed my dad's job loss, the medical bills from my mom's surgery, and attached documentation for all of it. The financial aid office wants to see receipts, termination letters, anything concrete.

Second, request a "professional judgment" review. Most people don't know this exists. A financial aid officer has the legal authority to override your EFC (Expected Family Contribution) based on special circumstances. You have to specifically ask for it.

Third, be persistent but polite. I emailed the office three times before I got a real response. Each time I followed up I attached the full documentation again so they wouldn't have to hunt for it.

Fourth, ask about emergency funds. My school had an emergency grant program I didn't know about — it covered one semester while my appeal was being reviewed.

The appeal process took 6 weeks. I got $2,800 of the $4,000 back. It's worth doing.

One thing I'd add — always appeal before the deadline they give you, but also know that even after the deadline, many schools will still process appeals if you have good documentation. Don't assume you've missed your window.

Reply from user firstgenstudent:
For Satisfactory Academic Progress (SAP) appeals specifically — if your aid was cut because your GPA dropped below the threshold — you need to also submit an academic plan signed by your advisor. The financial aid office won't tell you this upfront. Ask for the full checklist.
