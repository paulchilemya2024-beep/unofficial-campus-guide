Registration strategies that will save your semester:

Know your exact registration time. Don't approximate. Log into the student portal the night before and confirm your exact date and time. Set a phone alarm for 5 minutes before. Have your course list built and saved in advance so you're clicking add the second the clock hits.

Build a backup course list. Your top picks might fill within minutes. Have your second and third choices already found in the system so you can pivot instantly.

Check rate-your-professor sites before registering, but read critically. A professor with a 3.0 average who is rated as tough but fair and learned a lot is often better for you long-term than a 4.5 easy A professor who teaches you nothing.

Mix your schedule intentionally. Don't put all your hard classes in the same semester. If you're taking Organic Chemistry, don't also take Calculus 2 and a writing-intensive seminar. Spread the load.

Look for late-opening sections. Some departments release additional sections after the initial registration period. Check the schedule of classes every few days in the two weeks after registration opens.

Don't sleep on 8am classes. They fill last, which means you're more likely to get in. And fewer people are awake to bother you with questions during office hours.

Reply: Talk to your advisor before every registration period, not after. They know which courses are historically hard to get into, which professors you should avoid, and which requirements you might be forgetting.
