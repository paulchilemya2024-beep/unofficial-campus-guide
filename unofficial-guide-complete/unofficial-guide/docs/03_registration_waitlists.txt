Getting off a waitlist takes more work than people think. Here's what actually works:

Email the professor directly — not the department, the actual professor. Write something short: "I'm waitlisted for your [course name] section and genuinely interested in the material because [specific reason]. Is there any possibility of getting an add code or permission number if a spot opens?" Professors can often add students beyond the cap if they want to.

Show up the first day of class even if you're still on the waitlist. Sit in the front. Introduce yourself to the professor. Many students on the roster don't show up the first week — professors can give those spots to waitlisted students who demonstrate they actually want to be there.

Ask about department overrides. Some departments have a process where you can request an override of the enrollment cap. The department administrative coordinator (not the professor) usually handles this. Find their email on the department website and ask specifically for a "capacity override" or "add authorization."

Watch the enrollment numbers obsessively during add/drop. People drop courses constantly in the first two weeks. Set a phone alarm for 8am every day and check the registration system. When a spot opens, you need to add it within minutes — other waitlisted students are watching too.
