FAFSA opens October 1st every year. File it THE DAY IT OPENS. I cannot stress this enough. Financial aid is first-come, first-served for most institutional grants. Schools give out their own grant money until it's gone. If you file in February instead of October you might get the same federal aid but miss out on thousands in school-specific grants.

If you already missed the federal deadline (June 30th of the award year): File anyway. The federal deadline is for the award year but schools have their own priority deadlines that are usually much earlier (often February or March). Even after missing the priority deadline, filing late still qualifies you for federal loans and sometimes grants.

Common FAFSA mistakes:
- Not completing it because you think you "make too much" — always file, you never know what you qualify for
- Using the wrong tax year — FAFSA uses "prior-prior year" (two years back)
- Not adding all the schools you're considering — you can list up to 20
- Forgetting to sign with your FSA ID (both student AND parent need FSA IDs if you're dependent)

If your family's income changed significantly from the tax year on the form, contact your financial aid office. They can do a "special circumstances" adjustment using current year income instead.

Reply from user aidadvocate:
The Student Aid Report (SAR) you get after filing is not your award letter. Don't confuse them. The SAR just confirms your information was received and shows your EFC. Your actual award comes from each school separately.
