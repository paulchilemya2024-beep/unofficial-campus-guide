Mental health resources at college that most students don't know about:

Your campus counseling center (often called CAPS — Counseling and Psychological Services) almost always offers free sessions for currently enrolled students. Wait times can be long (2–4 weeks for a non-urgent appointment) so schedule earlier than you think you need to. Many schools offer same-day "urgent" appointments if you're in crisis — call and explicitly say you need urgent support.

Crisis resources that are free and immediate:
- 988 Suicide and Crisis Lifeline: call or text 988, available 24/7
- Crisis Text Line: text HOME to 741741
- Your campus likely has its own after-hours crisis line — Google "[your school] student crisis line"

Peer counseling programs: Many schools have trained student counselors who are free and often easier to see than a therapist. Less stigma, faster access. Search for "[your school] peer counseling" or "student wellness peer educators."

Student wellness centers often offer services beyond therapy: nutrition counseling, stress management workshops, meditation drop-ins, massage chairs (sounds silly but it's free stress relief). These are underused and walk-in friendly.

If you're on your parents' health insurance, you may be able to see an off-campus therapist at low cost. Look up "in-network" providers through your insurance portal. Some private therapists also offer student sliding scale rates — always ask.
