Campus resources most students never use but really should:

Campus food pantries: Almost every university has one. They're free, confidential, and require no proof of need at most schools. You just show up. Look up your school name plus food pantry — most are run by student affairs. Some also give out household supplies, toiletries, and winter clothing.

Emergency funds and emergency grants: Most schools have a small fund for students facing sudden financial hardship — unexpected medical bills, car repairs, sudden loss of income. The amounts vary from $200 to $2000 but can be life-saving. Email your Dean of Students office and ask specifically about emergency student funds. You won't be penalized for asking.

Textbook lending libraries: Many school libraries lend textbooks on course reserve. You might not be able to keep them overnight, but you can read them for free on campus. Also ask your professor if they have an extra desk copy — they often do.

Free legal services: Many universities offer free legal consultations through their law school clinic or student affairs office. Useful for lease disputes, traffic tickets, and understanding your rights as a tenant.

Student emergency housing: If you're experiencing housing instability or homelessness, contact the Dean of Students immediately. Schools have emergency housing protocols. This is more common than most people realize and there is no shame in using it.

Reply: I was too embarrassed to use the food pantry for two full semesters. When I finally went, the student running it was my classmate. Nobody cared. Nobody judged. Just go.
